SharkToothIsland.org Shark Tooth Island Field Guide
By Mozy Outdoors

Shark Tooth Island is one of those places that feels simple until you actually try to do it well.

You can absolutely paddle out and look for teeth, but the difference between a random trip and a really good one usually comes down to a few things: where you launch, when the tide is moving, how long to give yourself, what kind of shoreline to focus on, and what you bring with you.

That is why we made this field guide.

Created by Mozy Outdoors, this guide is designed to help first-timers and returning explorers alike have a better, smarter, more rewarding trip to Shark Tooth Island.

About This Project

A Mozy Outdoors
Field Project.

SharkToothIsland.org is part of a broader effort by Mozy Outdoors to make coastal exploration more understandable, practical, and engaging.

We are interested in more than just the find. We care about access, context, stewardship, and helping people enjoy places like this with a little more confidence.

This field guide is one part of that effort. The site itself is another — with live tide data, a species identification catalog, a megalodon size calculator, and more in the works.

Common Questions

FAQ

Is this actually free? expand_more
Yes. No catch, no upsell, no paywall. We built this guide because we wanted it to exist and thought others would find it useful too.
What format is the guide? expand_more
It's a downloadable PDF. Works on any device — phone, tablet, or printed out and tossed in a dry bag.
Will you spam me after I sign up? expand_more
No. You'll get the guide and occasional field notes from the station — tidal windows, new finds, and updates. That's it. No spam.
Can I visit Shark Tooth Island right now? expand_more
The island is accessible year-round by small boat, kayak, or paddlecraft. There's no ferry or public dock — you need your own way out there. The guide covers all the details.
Who made this? expand_more
Mozy Outdoors, based in the Cape Fear region of North Carolina. We build tools and resources for people who want to explore coastal environments with more confidence.
